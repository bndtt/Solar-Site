Frequently Asked Questions

Our AI provides estimates based on your location, electricity usage, and typical roof characteristics. For a final quote, we'll need to verify details about your specific roof. Most estimates are within 10-15% of final pricing.

The federal solar Investment Tax Credit (ITC) allows you to deduct 30% of your solar installation costs from your federal taxes. For a $20,000 system, that's $6,000 back. This credit is available through 2032.

The actual installation typically takes 1-3 days. However, the full process from signing to switch-on usually takes 2-3 months due to permits, utility approval, and scheduling.

If your roof is less than 10 years old, you're probably fine. If it's older, we recommend getting it inspected first. Solar panels last 25+ years, so you want a roof that'll last just as long.

We offer several options: cash purchase (best value), solar loans (own your system, $0 down), leases (lowest monthly payment), and PPAs (pay for power, not panels). We'll help you find what works best.

Your panels still produce some power on cloudy days (just less). At night, you draw from the grid. With net metering, the excess power you produce during the day credits your account for nighttime use. Add a battery to store power for outages or peak rates.
